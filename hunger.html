"If you can’t feed a hundred people, then feed just one."



India, with a population of over 1.3 billion, has seen tremendous growth in the past two decades. Gross Domestic Product has increased 4.5 times and per capita consumption has increased 3 times. Similarly, food grain production has increased almost 2 times. However, despite phenomenal industrial and economic growth and while India produces sufficient food to feed its population, it is unable to provide access to food to a large number of people, especially women and children. The Food and Agricultural Organisation (FAO) estimates nearly 40% of the food produced in India is lost or wasted every year due to inefficient supply chains. A lack of cooling and storage facilities in India also means 20% of the entire food production gets lost before it reaches the marketplace.690 million people across the world and 189.2 million in India don’t receive enough nutrition to lead an active, healthy life. In many restaurants,a lot of leftover food goes to the garbage bins. Instead of wasting that food,we can donate it to orphanages and homless people.Many people die because of hunger.When we have a chance to give them healthy food,it is wise to not waste it. The food you donate through us will save atleast a life. The satisfaction you get from feeding a person is heavenly. The war against hunger is truly mankind’s war of liberation. It is everyone's responsibility to participate in that war.
